SI 350: User Experience Field Research in the Public Sector

I was able to take a class the summer after my freshman year through the School of Information called SI 350: User Experience Research. This class was my first chance to learn what user experience is all about. I worked with a team of School of Information students with the Ann Arbor city government to solve a problem they were facing. Our problem was simple yet complex: find a way to integrate voice activated technology to make water billing and water billing updates fun and engaging, while keeping it secure. We conducted interviews with government employees, Ann Arbor residents, sent out surveys, built personas, and eventually created a deliverable to present to the Ann Arbor Government. We suggested rolling out voice activated technology slowly, by starting with water usage. Residents could ask how much water they had used so far this month, and how to cut down on water usage. The goal was that if this information is accurately reported to residents it will build trust, and hopefully lead to being able to make payments via voice activated technology.

University of Michigan Women's Baskteball Data Analytics

Since September of 2019, I have been working with the University of Michigan’s Athletics Department as a Data Analyst for the women’s basketball team. I have conducted various different types of research using both a Catapult device and heart rate monitor worn by all players. Catapult devices collect player load, which is an instantaneous measurement that tracks a player’s overall activity and movement. I then used this data to see if player performance could be predicted via heart rate or player load by calculating each player’s player efficiency rating (PER) for each game of the past season and compared those numbers. This March, I will be presenting my work at Michigan’s sports analytics symposium on Monday, March 23rd.
